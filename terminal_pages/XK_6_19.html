Subject: XK-6-19

Project Status: Terminated

Project Procedure:

XK-6-19's main body is to remain at the bottom of a 40ft x 40ft x 80ft chamber. 24,000 gallons of hydrocloric-acid is to be filled into the containment chamber and is to be replaced bi-weekly.
Excess growths must be removed by qualified personnel and is to be cauterized until a maximum of three growths remain.
XK-6-19 requires six-hundred pounds of high-protein meat every three days, although the meat can be supplemented by a mixture including.

  • 71% Carbon
  • 14.6% Calcium
  • 9% Phosphorus
  • 4% Potassium
  • 2% Sodium
  • 2% Chlorine
  • 1% Magnesium
  • .4% Sulfer

    • If XK-6-19 begins releasing memetic fluxuations that cross a harmonic resonance threshold of 3.4, a Quantitative-Resonance-Nullifier of Epsilon grade or higher will be deployed.
    A series of controlled shocks will then be applied starting at 120 Volts and 15 Amps, with a 10% increase in dosage every 15 seconds up to 300 Million Volts and 30,000 Amps.
    If XK-6-19 does not cease memetic emmisions, then growths are to removed until XK-6-19 is unable to output said emmisions.

    Description:

    XK-6-19 is a Bioengineered Memetic Cognitohazard. Its main body is a large mound of beige-white flesh with dark spots present in a 20:1 ratio on its surface area.
    When XK-6-19 is seeking nurishment, it will extrude several proboscises from the main body. The ends of the proboscises take the forms of elvoid skulls possessing a bite force of up to 3,700 psi.
    If XK-6-19 feels sufficiently threatened, then the main body will begin resonating at a frequency range of 15 to 0.1 Hertz. This frequency combined with a depression onto the Great Harmonics causes elvoid's within a range of three miles to forcefully disremember XK-6-19.
    Testing volunteers describe the sensation as "The mind slipping into the cracks while the body fills in whats left."
    XK-6-19 shows animalistic intelligence for all cognitive tests except for pattern recognition with ranking in the 99th percentile of subjects.
